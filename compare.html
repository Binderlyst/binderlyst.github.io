Binderlyst vs ManaBox vs Delver Lens vs Dragon Shield

Binderlyst is a free MTG card scanner and collection tracker for Android. This page compares it with three well-known alternatives: ManaBox, Delver Lens and Dragon Shield MTG Card Manager. Every claim about the other apps comes from that app's own Play Store listing or official website. Where we could not verify something, the table says "not checked" rather than guessing. Last checked: July 2026.

Where Binderlyst stands out

Everything in Binderlyst is free. No subscription, no in-app purchases, no scan or export limits, and no ads. Of the four apps here, it is the only one whose Play listing flags neither ads nor in-app purchases; Delver Lens, for example, caps free exports at roughly 105 cards and charges US$4 a month for the rest.

The scanner also does more on its own. Card language, promos and special art treatments are picked up automatically while you scan, and promo and treatment detection is something none of the other three listings claims at all. Collections track per-folder copy counts and seven condition grades with optional condition-adjusted prices, and the deck builder is format-aware, with legality checks that know which cards you own.

It is also the most private option here: no account, your collection stays on your device, and backups go to your own Google Drive rather than someone else's server. One thing to know before installing: Binderlyst is Android only. If that fits, it's on Google Play.
